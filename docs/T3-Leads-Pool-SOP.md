# T3 Leads Pool — Standard Operating Procedure

## Purpose

This document defines the daily expectations for T3 outbound agents working the shared leads pool. Every lead in the pool is unassigned. When you answer a call, you own the outcome — self-assign it, disposition it, and move on. This keeps the pool clean and gives you a direct path to writing premium.

---

## Daily KPI Targets

You are measured on 4 gates every day. Hit **3 of 4** to be compliant.

| Gate | Metric | Daily Target |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Volume** | Pool Dials | **≥ 150** |
| **Assignment** | Self-Assign Rate | **≥ 40%** of answered calls |
| **Count** | Self-Assigned Leads | **≥ 30** |
| **Quality** | Long Calls (15+ min) | **≥ 4** |

---

## How the Funnel Works

Every dial follows this path:

```
DIAL → ANSWER → SELF-ASSIGN → OUTCOME
                                 ├── DNC / Wrong Number    → removed from pool, no follow-up
                                 ├── Not Interested        → removed from pool, no follow-up
                                 ├── Callback Requested    → goes to YOUR follow-up queue
                                 ├── Presentation (15+ min)→ goes to YOUR active leads
                                 └── Sale Made             → submit app, moves to pipeline
```

### Your Expected Daily Funnel

| Step | Count | How |
|------|-------|-----|
| Dials | 150 | Minimum daily volume |
| Answered | ~75 | ~50% contact rate (dialer average) |
| Self-Assigned | 30 | 40% of answered — assign every contact regardless of outcome |
| Long Calls | 4 | Real conversations where you're qualifying or pitching |
| Sales | 1–4+ | Depends on your close rate — see projections below |

## Self-Assignment Rules

**Self-assign EVERY answered contact.** This is non-negotiable.

| Outcome | Action | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| DNC | Self-assign → mark DNC | Removes lead from rotation so nobody calls them again |
| Wrong Number | Self-assign → note it | Removes bad data from the pool |
| Not Interested | Self-assign → note reason | Stops the lead from being re-dialed tomorrow |
| Voicemail/No Answer | Do NOT assign | Lead stays in pool for future attempts |
| Callback Requested | Self-assign → set follow-up | Lead moves to your personal queue |
| Interested | Self-assign → schedule follow-up | Lead becomes your active prospect |
| Sale | Self-assign → submit application | Close it |

**Why this matters:** When you answer a call and DON'T self-assign, that person goes right back into the pool and gets called again by someone else. You wasted your time and theirs. Assigning every contact — even the bad ones — is what keeps the pool clean and productive for everyone.

## Daily Workflow

### Step 1: Work Your Personal Pipeline First
Before entering the pool, complete your follow-ups and work your call queue. Your personal pipeline always comes first.

### Step 2: Enter the Pool
Once your pipeline is worked, switch to the leads pool and start dialing. Target 150+ dials per session.

### Step 3: Assign Every Contact
For every answered call: have the conversation, then immediately self-assign and disposition the lead.

### Step 4: Hit Your Long Call Minimum
At least 4 calls should be 15+ minute presentations. If you're not getting to 4, you're hanging up too early on prospects who might be interested.

### Step 5: Work Your Self-Assigned Leads
Leads you pulled from the pool that need follow-up are now in your personal queue. Work them on your own schedule, independent of future pool sessions.
