# T2 Hybrid — Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026

Your daily playbook — what to do, when to do it, and what your numbers should look like.

---

## The Three Activity Channels

### Channel 1: Live Inbound Calls

You receive **7 inbound leads per day** through the ICD. Each call costs the company ~$83. When you close a deal, you earn **3 bonus inbound leads** that day — up to 10 total.


| Metric                 | Expectation                |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Base inbound leads/day | 7                          |
| Bonus leads (on close) | +3 (up to 10 total)        |
| Close rate (inbound)   | 20-25%                     |
| Expected IB sales/day  | 1.4-1.8                    |
| Disposition            | Immediate after every call |


**When you connect:** Qualify, present, and close on the first call whenever possible. If the prospect needs time, set a follow-up appointment within 24-48 hours.

**Every call gets one of three outcomes:**

1. **Sale** — submit the application, earn 3 bonus leads
2. **Follow-up set** — appointment within 24-48 hours, noted in CRM
3. **Closed out** — DNC, not interested, wrong number — disposition and move on

**The bonus incentive:** Closing early in the day earns you 3 more inbound leads. This rewards agents who present with urgency on every call. An agent who closes on their first call could work 10 inbound leads instead of 7.

### Channel 2: Follow-Up Appointments

Scheduled callbacks from prior inbound calls AND from leads pool contacts. You call manually at the scheduled time.

**The 1-5 Close rule:** Set every appointment within 24-48 hours. Interest fades fast. Close within 1-5 total contacts.


| Metric              | Expectation                       |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Daily follow-ups    | 5-15 (grows with pipeline + pool) |
| Dials per follow-up | 1 attempt at scheduled time       |
| Expected talk time  | 30-90 min                         |
| Past due tolerance  | **0**                             |


**If they answer:** Work the call — present, close, or reschedule within 24-48 hours.

**If they don't answer:** Sticky note in CRM with context. Set a new follow-up for the next business day. After 3 missed appointments on the same lead, close it out.

**Past due must be zero.** Follow-ups from inbound calls are $83 leads. Follow-ups from pool contacts are tomorrow's pipeline. Both matter.

### Channel 3: Leads Pool

Shared leads — nobody owns them. You enter a pool session, dial, and self-assign every answered contact to pull them out of the pool.

**Key difference from T3:** Pool is your secondary channel, not your primary. Inbound calls take priority. Pool fills the gaps between inbound calls and builds your follow-up pipeline.


| Metric               | Expectation            |
| -------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Pool dials           | 40-70/day              |
| Expected talk time   | 30-60 min              |
| Long calls (15+ min) | ≥ 2/day                |
| Self-assign rate     | ≥ 30% of pool answered |


**Contact rate** = % of pool dials where someone answers.

**Voicemail rule:** Hang up and move on. You don't own pool leads. If you must leave a message, use the **automatic VM drop**. Manual voicemails inflate your talk time and contact rate.

**Close rate impact:** Sales from leads pool do NOT count against your inbound close rate. Pool is additive production — it can only help your numbers, never hurt them.

## Daily Workflow

### Day at a Glance (CST)


| Time               | Duration    | Activity                                         |
| ------------------ | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 8:00 - 9:00 AM     | 1 hr        | Team meeting / training                          |
| 9:00 - 9:30 AM     | 30 min      | **Block 1** — Follow-up appointments             |
| 9:30 AM - 12:00 PM | 2.5 hr      | **Block 2** — Inbound calls + pool between calls |
| 12:00 - 12:30 PM   | 30 min      | Lunch                                            |
| 12:30 - 2:30 PM    | 2 hr        | **Block 2** — Inbound calls + pool between calls |
| 2:30 - 2:45 PM     | 15 min      | Break                                            |
| 2:45 - 4:30 PM     | 1 hr 45 min | **Block 3** — Pool session                       |
| 4:30 - 5:00 PM     | 30 min      | **Block 4** — Pipeline review + wrap-up          |


**Production time:** 7 hours

---

### Block 1 — Follow-Up Appointments (9:00 - 9:30 AM)

- Check task list, call each scheduled appointment — one attempt
- If they answer: work the call
- If no answer: sticky note + reschedule for tomorrow (or close out after 3rd missed attempt)
- **Expected:** 5-15 dials, 30-90 min talk time

### Block 2 — Inbound Calls + Pool Fill (9:30 AM - 2:30 PM)

- Take inbound calls as they come through ICD
- Between inbound calls, enter the leads pool and dial
- Self-assign every answered pool contact
- Disposition everything immediately
- **Expected:** 7-10 inbound leads, 20-30 pool dials during gaps

### Block 3 — Pool Session (2:45 - 4:30 PM)

- Dedicated pool time after inbound volume typically slows
- Dial pool leads, self-assign every answered contact
- Target 2+ long calls (15+ min)
- Hang up on voicemails, move on
- **Expected:** 20-40 pool dials, 20-40 min talk time

### Block 4 — Pipeline Review + Wrap-Up (4:30 - 5:00 PM)

- Reattempt any follow-ups missed in Block 1
- Review pipeline: close out leads with 3+ missed contacts
- Set up tomorrow's task list
- **Expected:** Clean pipeline, zero past due going into tomorrow

## Self-Assignment Rules (Pool)

Self-assign **every** answered pool contact.


| Outcome               | Action                           | Why                                    |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| DNC                   | Self-assign → mark DNC           | Removes lead from rotation             |
| Wrong Number          | Self-assign → note it            | Removes bad data from pool             |
| Not Interested        | Self-assign → note reason        | Stops the lead from being re-dialed    |
| Voicemail / No Answer | Do NOT assign                    | Lead stays in pool for future attempts |
| Callback Requested    | Self-assign → set follow-up date | Lead moves to YOUR appointments        |
| Interested            | Self-assign → schedule follow-up | Lead becomes your prospect             |
| Sale                  | Self-assign → submit application | Close it                               |

## Follow-Up Discipline

Follow-ups come from two sources: inbound calls and pool self-assigns. Both create obligations.


| Follow-Up Age    | Status          | Action                                                         |
| ---------------- | --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scheduled today  | Due             | Call at the scheduled time                                     |
| 1 day past due   | Overdue         | Call immediately in Block 1                                    |
| 2 days past due  | Escalation risk | Must be worked today or closed out                             |
| 3+ days past due | Non-compliant   | Subject to pipeline audit action (see Pipeline Discipline SOP) |


### Close-Out Rules


| Signal                                   | Action                                                      |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 3 missed appointments                    | Close out — interest is gone                                |
| DNC / wrong number                       | Close out immediately                                       |
| Explicitly declined                      | Close out, note reason                                      |
| No answer after 3 attempts across 3 days | Close out                                                   |
| Still interested but timing is wrong     | Reschedule 1-2 weeks out, then close out if still no answer |

## Pipeline Health

Your pipeline grows from two channels — inbound follow-ups and pool self-assigns. Keep it moving.


| Pipeline Size           | Status       | Action                                              |
| ----------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| 0-10 active follow-ups  | Light        | Normal for T2 — keep working pool to build pipeline |
| 10-25 active follow-ups | Healthy      | Balanced pipeline                                   |
| 25-40 active follow-ups | Accumulating | Review and close out stale leads                    |
| 40+ active follow-ups   | Overloaded   | Reduce pool time, clear pipeline first              |

## The Hybrid Advantage

T2 is the only tier with access to both inbound leads and the leads pool. Used correctly, this is a significant advantage:

- **Inbound** gives you high-intent, warm leads — close them aggressively
- **Pool** fills gaps between inbound calls and generates tomorrow's follow-ups
- **Follow-ups** compound — today's pool self-assigns become tomorrow's callbacks
- **Close rate is protected** — pool activity is tracked separately, so working pool can only help you

The agents who succeed in T2 are the ones who treat pool time as pipeline investment, not filler.

## Red Flags


| Pattern                       | Signal                            | What It Means                              |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Past due rising               | Past due > 0 and growing          | Not working follow-ups — revenue bleeding  |
| IB close rate dropping        | Below 15% for 5+ days             | Call quality issue — review presentations  |
| Zero pool dials               | 0 pool activity for 3+ days       | Not building pipeline — future revenue gap |
| Pipeline growing, no closings | 30+ active leads, flat sales      | Hoarding leads without working them        |
| Short IB talk times           | Avg < 5 min/call                  | Not presenting — rushing through calls     |
| Pool without follow-through   | High pool dials, 0 follow-ups set | Dialing pool without converting contacts   |
