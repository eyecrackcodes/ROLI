# Agent Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026
**Applies to:** All agents — Remote & Charlotte

---

## How This Works

Every agent operates under the same model. You receive the same inbound lead allocation, have access to the leads pool, and are held to the same pipeline and conversion standards.

The company invests significantly in every lead that lands on your screen. Your job is to work those leads thoroughly, keep your pipeline clean, and use the pool to build additional opportunities. This SOP defines exactly what that looks like.

---

| Item | Standard |

Agents who maintain a clean pipeline and hit close rate targets may request additional leads beyond 7/day.

## Pipeline Discipline

Pipeline management is the single most important operational discipline. A clean pipeline protects the investment made in your leads and directly protects your commission income.

### Pipeline Standards

| Standard | Target | Hard Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Past due follow-ups | 0 | 0 (always) |
| Pipeline size | ≤ 20 leads | 30 leads max |
| Pipeline age (oldest lead) | < 7 days | 14 days max |
| Disposition rate | 100% same-day | 100% (no exceptions) |
| Follow-up set rate | Every interested contact | Every interested contact |

### What a Clean Pipeline Looks Like

| Pipeline Size | Status | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 20 | Healthy | Room for inbound + pool follow-ups — keep going |
| 21–30 | At capacity | Work existing leads before adding more |
| 31–40 | Over limit | Clear pipeline before pool or additional inbound |
| > 40 | Critical | Stop all new activity, work pipeline down immediately |

### Why Past Due Follow-Ups Matter

Follow-up leads convert at **2–3x the rate** of first contacts because the prospect already expressed interest. Every past due follow-up is a warm lead going cold — and commission walking out the door.

| Past Due | What It Means | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Clean — you're on track | Keep going |
| 1–3 | Warm leads getting cold | Catch up immediately |
| 4+ | Commission at risk | Stop pool, work follow-ups only |

### Priority Order (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Follow-up appointments** — work all scheduled callbacks at their scheduled time
2. **Past due follow-ups** — clear to zero before anything else
3. **Stale pipeline** — close out or withdraw leads older than 14 days
4. **Inbound calls** — take your allocated leads
5. **Pool sessions** — supplemental activity to build tomorrow's pipeline

If your pipeline is dirty, you do not enter the pool. Period.

## Leads Pool

The leads pool is a shared set of unassigned leads available to every agent. Pool activity is **supplemental to inbound production** — it fills gaps between inbound calls and builds your follow-up pipeline for the next day.

Pool leads cost you nothing extra. Every pool sale is additional commission on top of what you earn from inbound.

### Pool Targets

| Metric | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|---|---|---|
| Find follow-up-worthy contacts | 5 per day | — |
| Pool sales | — | 1 per week |
| Self-assign rate | ≥ 30% of answered calls | — |

### Pool Workflow

1. Complete all follow-up appointments first
2. Confirm pipeline is clean (zero past due, ≤ 30 leads)
3. Enter pool session during designated blocks
4. Dial pool leads
5. **Self-assign every answered contact** — including not-interested and DNC — to remove them from rotation
6. Set follow-up appointments for interested contacts
7. Target: find 5 contacts worth following up with

### Why Self-Assigning Matters

Every un-assigned answered contact stays in the pool and gets dialed again by another agent. That wastes everyone's time. Self-assigning:

- Removes the contact from rotation (no more repeat dials by other agents)
- Creates a follow-up trail (you own the relationship)
- Feeds tomorrow's pipeline (today's pool contact is tomorrow's warm callback)

### Self-Assign Rate

| Assign Rate | Status |
|---|---|
| ≥ 50% | Excellent — fully cleaning pool contacts |
| 30–49% | Compliant — pool is moving, discipline in place |
| < 30% | Non-compliant — contacts recycling back into pool |

### Pool Quality

Your pool work is measured on quality, not just volume. Two metrics matter:

**Pool Efficiency** (talk time ÷ pool dials) — are you actually making real calls?

| Efficiency | Status |
|---|---|
| 0.5–1 min/dial | Healthy |
| 0.3–0.5 min/dial | Fast but acceptable |
| < 0.3 min/dial | Flagged — too fast to be making real contact |
| > 1 min/dial | Too slow — tighten up workflow |

**Presentation Rate** (15+ min calls ÷ self-assigned leads) — are pool contacts turning into real sales conversations?

| Pres Rate | Status |
|---|---|
| ≥ 20% | Strong — converting contacts into pitch conversations |
| 12–19% | Acceptable — room to improve on qualifying |
| < 12% | Weak — assigning but not engaging meaningfully |

## Close Rate & Lead Eligibility

Close rate determines whether you earn additional inbound leads or face quality review. It's calculated weekly as total sales ÷ total leads delivered.

| Threshold | Weekly CR | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Target | ≥ 15% | Eligible for additional leads beyond 7/day |
| Floor | 10–14% | Standard allocation — focus on converting existing pipeline |
| Warning | 7–9% | Quality review, may be restricted to 5 leads/day |
| Crisis | < 7% for 3+ days | Call quality audit, presentation coaching required |

### What Close Rate Means for Your Income

At 30% commission on an average premium of ~$1,200:

| Close Rate | Sales per Week (from 35 leads) | Weekly Commission | Monthly Commission |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20% | 7 | $2,520 | ~$10,900 |
| 15% (target) | 5.25 | $1,890 | ~$8,200 |
| 10% (floor) | 3.5 | $1,260 | ~$5,460 |
| 7% (crisis) | 2.45 | $882 | ~$3,820 |

Add pool income on top: 1 pool sale per week at $1,200 avg premium = ~$360/week ($1,560/month) in extra commission.

**Total at target: ~$9,700/month from inbound + pool combined ($116k annualized).**

## What Happens When Standards Aren't Met

Problems are addressed progressively. No one gets docked on a first offense. But repeated non-compliance has real consequences.

### Level 1: Coaching Conversation

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 0 for 2 consecutive business days |
| **What happens** | 1-on-1 with your manager. Review pipeline together, identify missed leads, create a catch-up plan. |
| **Timeline** | Same day it's identified. |

### Level 2: Written Warning

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 3 for 3+ consecutive days, OR Level 1 repeated within 2 weeks |
| **What happens** | Formal written warning. You sign acknowledgment and receive specific improvement targets. |
| **Timeline** | Next business day after identification. |

### Level 3: Lead Reduction

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 5 after written warning, OR 3+ warnings in 30 days |
| **What happens** | Lead allocation drops from 7 to 5 per day. Pool access suspended. |
| **Restored when** | Pipeline hits zero past due for 3 consecutive business days. |
| **Why** | If you can't work 7, you shouldn't receive 7. Fewer leads means less commission — clearing your pipeline is the fastest way to get back to full allocation. |

### Level 4: Pipeline Sweep

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Pipeline > 40 after written warning |
| **What happens** | Management reviews your pipeline and closes out or redistributes leads you should have worked. |
| **Timeline** | During the weekly pipeline audit (Wednesdays). |
| **Impact** | Swept leads are given to other agents or returned to the pool. Those are potential sales — and commissions — that you lose. |

### Level 5: Performance Review

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Sustained non-compliance after Level 3+4, OR 4+ warnings in 60 days |
| **What happens** | Formal performance improvement plan (PIP). May include pay impact or termination discussion. |
| **Timeline** | Within 5 business days of identification. |

## Weekly Pipeline Audit

Management conducts a pipeline review every Wednesday. This is when issues become formally documented and escalation begins if standards aren't being met.

**What's reviewed:**

1. Past due = 0
2. Past due trend (improving or getting worse)
3. Pipeline ≤ 30
4. No leads older than 14 days without activity
5. 100% same-day disposition
6. Leads with 3+ missed appointments closed out

**Scoring:**

- 6/6: Clean — no action needed
- 4–5/6: Minor issues — coaching conversation, fix by next audit
- 2–3/6: Significant issues — written warning
- 0–1/6: Critical — escalation begins immediately

## Missed Calls & Pool Follow-Ups

All missed inbound calls are visible to you. You should:

1. Review missed calls daily
2. Find 5 contacts per day from pool / missed calls worth following up with
3. Set follow-up appointments for qualified contacts
4. Target 1 additional sale per week from pool/missed call follow-ups

These are warm contacts who already called in. They're some of the easiest sales you'll find — don't leave them sitting.
