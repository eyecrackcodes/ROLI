# T1 Inbound — Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026

Your daily playbook — what to do, when to do it, and what your numbers should look like.

---

## The Two Activity Channels

### Channel 1: Live Inbound Calls

You receive **10 inbound leads per day** through the ICD. Each call costs the company ~$83. These are the most expensive leads on the floor — every one matters.


| Metric             | Expectation                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| Inbound leads/day  | 10                           |
| Close rate         | 25-30%                       |
| Expected sales/day | 2.5-3.0                      |
| Talk time per call | Varies — work the call fully |
| Disposition        | Immediate after every call   |


**When you connect:** Qualify, present, and close on the first call whenever possible. If the prospect needs time, set a follow-up appointment within 24-48 hours.

**Every call gets one of three outcomes:**

1. **Sale** — submit the application
2. **Follow-up set** — appointment within 24-48 hours, noted in CRM
3. **Closed out** — DNC, not interested, wrong number — disposition and move on

No call should end without a clear next step documented in the CRM.

### Channel 2: Follow-Up Appointments

Scheduled callbacks from prior inbound calls. You call manually at the scheduled time — these are not in the dialer.

**The 1-5 Close rule:** Every appointment should be set within 24-48 hours of the initial call. Interest fades fast. Close within 1-5 total contacts.


| Metric              | Expectation                         |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Daily follow-ups    | 3-10 (grows with pipeline maturity) |
| Dials per follow-up | 1 attempt at scheduled time         |
| Expected talk time  | 30-90 min                           |
| Past due tolerance  | **0**                               |


**If they answer:** Pick up where you left off — present, close, or reschedule within 24-48 hours.

**If they don't answer:** Sticky note in CRM with context. Set a new follow-up attempt for the next business day. After 3 total missed appointments on the same lead, close it out — the interest is gone.

**Past due must be zero.** These are $83 leads that already showed interest. Every missed follow-up is money left on the table.

**Production time:** 7 hours

---

### Block 1 — Follow-Up Appointments (9:00 - 9:30 AM)

- Check task list, call each scheduled appointment — one attempt
- If they answer: work the call
- If no answer: sticky note + reschedule for tomorrow (or close out after 3rd missed attempt)
- **Expected:** 3-10 dials, 30-90 min talk time

### Block 2 — Live Inbound Calls (9:30 AM - 4:30 PM)

- Take inbound calls as they come through ICD
- Qualify, present, close or set follow-up on every call
- Disposition immediately after each call
- **Expected:** 10 inbound leads, 25-30% close rate

### Block 3 — Follow-Up Catch-Up + Pipeline Review (4:30 - 5:00 PM)

- Reattempt any follow-ups missed in Block 1
- Review pipeline: clear any leads that should be closed out
- Set up tomorrow's task list
- **Expected:** Clean pipeline, zero past due going into tomorrow

## Follow-Up Discipline

Every inbound call that results in a follow-up appointment creates an obligation. You set the appointment — you work it.


| Follow-Up Age    | Status          | Action                                                         |
| ---------------- | --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scheduled today  | Due             | Call at the scheduled time                                     |
| 1 day past due   | Overdue         | Call immediately in Block 1                                    |
| 2 days past due  | Escalation risk | Must be worked today or closed out                             |
| 3+ days past due | Non-compliant   | Subject to pipeline audit action (see Pipeline Discipline SOP) |


### The Math on Neglect

Each inbound lead costs ~$83. At a 25% close rate, every 4 leads produces 1 sale. At $1,150 avg premium:


| Past Due Leads | Revenue at Risk |
| -------------- | --------------- |
| 1              | $288            |
| 3              | $863            |
| 5              | $1,438          |
| 10             | $2,875          |


These aren't cold leads — they're people who already talked to you and agreed to a callback. The close rate on follow-ups is higher than on first contact. Neglecting them is the most expensive mistake you can make.

## Pipeline Health

Your pipeline should be small and active. Unlike T2 and T3 agents who manage large queues, your pipeline is built entirely from inbound conversations.


| Pipeline Size           | Status       | Action                                             |
| ----------------------- | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
| 0-5 active follow-ups   | Light        | Normal — you're closing or closing out efficiently |
| 5-15 active follow-ups  | Healthy      | Manageable — keep working them down                |
| 15-25 active follow-ups | Accumulating | Review and close out leads with 3+ missed contacts |
| 25+ active follow-ups   | Overloaded   | Stop accepting new leads until pipeline is cleared |


### Close-Out Rules

Not every follow-up will convert. Knowing when to close out a lead is as important as knowing when to follow up.


| Signal                                   | Action                                                      |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 3 missed appointments                    | Close out — interest is gone                                |
| DNC / wrong number                       | Close out immediately                                       |
| Explicitly declined                      | Close out, note reason                                      |
| No answer after 3 attempts across 3 days | Close out                                                   |
| Still interested but timing is wrong     | Reschedule 1-2 weeks out, then close out if still no answer |

## Red Flags


| Pattern                  | Signal                          | What It Means                                    |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Past due rising          | Past due > 0 and growing        | Not working follow-ups — revenue bleeding        |
| Close rate dropping      | Below 20% for 5+ days           | Call quality issue — review presentations        |
| Pipeline growing         | 20+ active leads, no closings   | Hoarding leads instead of closing or closing out |
| Short talk times         | Avg < 5 min/call on inbound     | Not presenting — rushing through calls           |
| Low follow-up conversion | Follow-ups set but never closed | Setting appointments as a stall tactic           |
