# Pipeline & Leads Pool — Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026
**Model:** Unified Flat Model — all agents, all sites

---

## Why This Document Exists

Every lead costs money. Every missed follow-up is wasted money. This SOP defines how agents manage their pipeline and use the leads pool — and what happens when they don't.

### The Marketing Investment Behind Every Lead

These numbers come from actual daily marketing spend data (source: `company_daily_metrics`).

| Metric | Current Average | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Daily marketing spend (org-wide) | $28,000 | Company Dashboard |
| Active agents | 60 | ROLI roster |
| Daily marketing cost per agent | ~$467 | Spend ÷ agents |
| Cost per call/lead (CPC) | $57 | Company Dashboard |
| Cost per sale | $429 | Company Dashboard |
| Avg premium per sale | $1,208 | Company Dashboard |
| Org sales rate | 19.6% | Company Dashboard |
| ROAS (return on ad spend) | 2.94× | Premium ÷ spend |
| Marketing as % of premium | 25% | Company Dashboard |

**What this means for each agent:**

Every agent receives 7 inbound leads per day. That's **$399 in marketing spend** just to put those leads in front of you. If you close 1 deal (the daily target), you generate ~$1,208 in premium against ~$467 in marketing cost. That's healthy.

If you let 5 leads go past due, that's **$285 in marketing dollars sitting idle** — and ~$1,812 in potential premium rotting in your pipeline ($1,208 × 30% follow-up close rate × 5).

## Pipeline Standards

### The Rules

| Standard | Target | Hard Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Past due follow-ups | 0 | 0 (always) |
| Pipeline size | ≤ 20 leads | 30 leads max |
| Pipeline age (oldest lead) | < 7 days | 14 days max |
| Disposition rate | 100% same-day | 100% (no exceptions) |
| Follow-up set rate | Every interested contact | Every interested contact |

### What a Clean Pipeline Looks Like

| Pipeline Size | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 20 | Healthy | Room for inbound + pool follow-ups |
| 21–30 | At capacity | Work existing leads before adding more |
| 31–40 | Over limit | Clear pipeline before pool or additional inbound |
| > 40 | Critical | Stop all new activity, pipeline down immediately |

### Past Due = Money Burning

| Past Due Count | Marketing $ Idle | Premium at Risk | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | $0 | $0 | Clean — keep going |
| 1–3 | $57–$171 | $362–$1,087 | Catch up immediately |
| 4–5 | $228–$285 | $1,450–$1,812 | Stop pool, work follow-ups only |
| 10+ | $570+ | $3,624+ | Management intervention |

Past due premium at risk = past due count × $1,208 avg premium × 30% follow-up close rate.

### Priority Order (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Follow-up appointments** — work all scheduled callbacks at their scheduled time
2. **Past due follow-ups** — clear to zero before anything else
3. **Stale pipeline** — close out or withdraw leads older than 14 days
4. **Inbound calls** — take your allocated leads
5. **Pool sessions** — supplemental activity to build tomorrow's pipeline

If your pipeline is dirty, you do not enter the pool. Period.

## Leads Pool Standards

### What the Pool Is

The leads pool is a shared set of unassigned leads. Any agent can enter a pool session, dial pool leads, and self-assign answered contacts. Self-assigning is the only way a lead leaves the pool.

Pool activity is **supplemental to inbound production** — it fills gaps between inbound calls and builds your follow-up pipeline for the next day.

### Pool Targets

| Metric | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|---|---|---|
| Find follow-up-worthy contacts | 5 per day | — |
| Pool sales | — | 1 per week |
| Self-assign rate | ≥ 30% of answered calls | — |

### Pool Workflow

1. Complete all follow-up appointments first
2. Confirm pipeline is clean (zero past due, ≤ 30 leads)
3. Enter pool session during designated blocks (see Daily Schedule)
4. Dial pool leads
5. **Self-assign every answered contact** — including not-interested and DNC — to remove them from rotation
6. Set follow-up appointments for interested contacts
7. Target: find 5 contacts worth following up with

### Why Self-Assigning Matters

Every un-assigned answered contact stays in the pool and gets dialed again by another agent. That's wasted time across the entire floor. Self-assigning:

- Removes the contact from rotation (no more wasted dials)
- Creates a follow-up trail (you own the relationship)
- Feeds tomorrow's pipeline (today's pool contact is tomorrow's warm callback)

### Self-Assign Rate Thresholds

| Assign Rate | Status | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ 50% | Excellent | Fully cleaning pool contacts |
| 30–49% | Compliant | Pool is moving, discipline in place |
| < 30% | Non-compliant | Contacts recycling back into pool, wasting other agents' time |

### Pool Efficiency (Gaming Detection)

Pool efficiency = talk time minutes ÷ pool dials. This catches agents who rapid-skip through pool leads without making real calls.

| Efficiency | Status | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| > 1 min/dial | Too slow | Distracted, over-noting, idle time between dials |
| 0.5–1 min/dial | Healthy | Realistic workflow |
| 0.3–0.5 min/dial | Fast | Acceptable if assign rate is compliant |
| < 0.3 min/dial | Flagged | Likely gaming — rapid-skipping without real contact |

### Presentation Rate (Pool Quality)

Presentation rate = long calls (15+ min) ÷ self-assigned leads. Measures whether pool contacts are turning into real sales conversations, not just quick dispositions.

| Pres Rate | Status | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ 20% | Strong | Converting contacts into pitch conversations |
| 12–19% | Acceptable | Room to improve on qualifying |
| < 12% | Weak | Assigning but not engaging meaningfully |

## Daily Schedule (CST)

| Block | Time | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 9:00–9:30 | Work follow-up appointments (scheduled callbacks) |
| 2 | 9:30–12:00 | Inbound calls + pool sessions |
| 3 | 12:00–1:00 | Lunch |
| 4 | 1:00–4:30 | Inbound calls + pool sessions |
| 5 | 4:30–5:00 | Pipeline cleanup, notes, next-day prep |

Follow-up appointments always take priority. If you have a callback scheduled for 2:00 PM, you stop pool/inbound and make that call at 2:00 PM.

| # | Gate | Target | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Past Due | 0 | Zero | Any > 0 |

## Close Rate & Lead Eligibility

Close rate determines whether an agent gets additional inbound leads or faces quality review.

| Threshold | Weekly CR | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Target | ≥ 15% | Eligible for additional leads beyond 7/day |
| Floor | 10–14% | Standard allocation, focus on converting existing pipeline |
| Warning | 7–9% | Review quality, may restrict to 5/day |
| Crisis | < 7% for 3+ days | Quality audit — call review, presentation coaching |

At the org's current $57 CPC, a 15% close rate means each sale costs ~$380 in lead spend. A 10% close rate pushes cost-per-sale to $570. Below 7%, each sale costs over $814 — destroying the ROAS for the entire floor.

### The Math

| Close Rate | Leads to Make 1 Sale | Marketing Cost per Sale | Premium per Sale | Net Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20% | 5 | $285 | $1,208 | $923 |
| 15% (target) | 6.7 | $380 | $1,208 | $828 |
| 10% (floor) | 10 | $570 | $1,208 | $638 |
| 7% (crisis) | 14.3 | $814 | $1,208 | $394 |
| 5% | 20 | $1,140 | $1,208 | $68 |

Below 7%, the agent is barely covering marketing costs. Below 5%, the org loses money on every lead delivered to that agent.

## Implications — Escalation Framework

Problems are addressed progressively. No one gets docked on a first offense. But repeated non-compliance has consequences tied directly to the marketing investment.

### Level 1: Coaching Conversation

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 0 for 2 consecutive business days |
| **Action** | 1-on-1 with manager. Review pipeline, identify missed leads, create catch-up plan. |
| **Timeline** | Same day the system flags it. |
| **Marketing impact** | $114+ in lead spend going stale (2+ leads × $57). |

### Level 2: Written Warning

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 3 for 3+ consecutive days, OR Level 1 repeated within 2 weeks |
| **Action** | Formal written warning. Agent signs acknowledgment. Specific improvement targets set. |
| **Timeline** | Next business day after identification. |
| **Marketing impact** | $171+ in direct lead spend at risk, plus $1,087+ in potential premium. |

### Level 3: Lead Allocation Reduction

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Past due > 5 after written warning, OR chronic non-compliance (3+ warnings in 30 days) |
| **Action** | Drop from 7 to 5 inbound leads/day. Suspend pool access. |
| **Timeline** | Effective immediately. Restored when pipeline hits zero past due for 3 consecutive days. |
| **Marketing impact** | Org is spending $467/day on an agent who can't work what they have. Reduction redirects ~$114/day of lead spend to higher-performing agents. |
| **Rationale** | If you can't work 7, you don't get 7. |

### Level 4: Pipeline Sweep

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Pipeline > 40 after written warning |
| **Action** | Management reviews the pipeline and forcibly withdraws/closes out leads the agent should have handled. |
| **Timeline** | During the weekly audit (every Wednesday). |
| **Marketing impact** | Swept leads are redistributed to agents who will work them, or returned to the pool. Every day a lead sits unworked past 14 days, the conversion probability drops by ~50%. |

### Level 5: Performance Review

| | |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Sustained non-compliance after Level 3+4, OR 4+ warnings in 60 days |
| **Action** | Formal performance improvement plan (PIP). May include pay impact or termination discussion. |
| **Timeline** | Within 5 business days of identification. |
| **Marketing impact** | At 7 leads/day × 22 working days, a chronically non-compliant agent wastes ~$8,800/month in marketing spend on leads that will never be properly worked. |

## Weekly Pipeline Audit (Every Wednesday)

Management runs through this checklist for every agent using the ROLI Pipeline Intelligence dashboard. Takes 15-20 minutes per site.

| # | Check | Source | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Past due = 0 | ROLI Pipeline Dashboard | Zero | Any > 0 |
| 2 | Past due trend | d/d delta from prior week | Flat or declining | Rising |
| 3 | Pipeline ≤ 30 | pipeline_compliance_daily | Within limit | Over 30 |
| 4 | No leads older than 14 days | Pipeline age distribution | All < 14 days | Any ≥ 14 days |
| 5 | Disposition rate = 100% | Same-day disposition | 100% | < 100% |
| 6 | Close-out discipline | Leads with 3+ missed appts | All closed out | Any still open |

**Scoring:**

- 6/6: Clean pipeline — no action needed
- 4-5/6: Minor issues — coaching conversation, fix by next audit
- 2-3/6: Significant issues — written warning
- 0-1/6: Critical — escalate to Level 3+ immediately
