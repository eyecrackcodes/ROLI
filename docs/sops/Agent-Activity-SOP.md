# Agent Daily Activity SOP

**Effective:** April 2026
**Applies to:** All active agents
**Purpose:** A simple, clear playbook for what a great day looks like. Hit these activities consistently and the production takes care of itself.

---

## The Day in One Sentence

> Work your follow-ups first, take your 7 inbound leads, then pull from the pool to build tomorrow's pipeline.

That's it. Everything below is just detail on those three things.

## How the Math Works

Before the targets, here's the system you're plugged into. Once you see how the pieces fit, the daily numbers below make sense.

**The cadence:** Every new inbound lead enters a **6-touch dialer cadence** that runs over about **8–9 business days**. The system spaces the touches automatically — Day 0 (×2), Day 1, Day 3, Day 6, Day 10 — so you don't have to chase each lead manually.

**Steady-state queue size:** With 7 new leads/day living in cadence for ~9 days, your active lead queue holds about **60 leads** at any time. That's normal and healthy.

**Daily workload from the queue:** 60 leads × 6 touches ÷ 9 days ≈ **40 dial attempts per day** flowing out of the cadence. Add your 7 live inbound conversations and pool sessions in the gaps and you've got a full, productive day.

**The conversion path:** 7 leads × 6 touches gets you contact with ~65% of them = ~4–5 real conversations per day's batch. Convert ~25% of those and you're at the **1 sale/day** target (≈ 15% close rate on leads delivered).

**The activity floor:** The whole day rolls up into one number — **300 RPAs (Revenue Producing Activity minutes) per day** at minimum. That's 5 hours of queue-ready time, dial time, and talk time combined. See the next section for what counts and how it's measured.

The whole model is designed so the cadence does the heavy lifting in the background and you focus on (a) live inbound conversations, (b) honoring the follow-ups you've set, and (c) keeping the small backlog of untouched and past-due leads at zero.

## RPAs — Your Activity Floor

**RPA = Revenue Producing Activity.** It's the simplest measure of whether you actually showed up and worked today. One RPA = one minute of activity that produces revenue (or sets up the revenue).

### What Counts as an RPA

| Activity | Counts as RPA? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Queue-ready time** (logged into the dialer, available for inbound) | ✅ Yes | This is the largest RPA bucket on most days — you're earning your inbound allocation |
| **Talk time** (live conversation, inbound or outbound) | ✅ Yes | The most valuable RPA — every minute is direct selling |
| **Outbound dial time** (each dial × ~30 sec for ring + connect) | ✅ Yes | Cadence dials, follow-up calls, manual dials — all counted |
| **Pool session dial + talk time** | ✅ Yes | Counted exactly the same way |
| Bathroom break, water, stretch | ❌ No | Normal — that's why the floor is 5 hours, not 8 |
| Lunch | ❌ No | 1-hour block carved out of the day |
| Admin (notes, dispositions, calendar setup) | ❌ No | Necessary, but not RPA |
| Coaching, team meeting, training | ❌ No | Counted separately |
| **Dials placed via Dialpad** | ❌ No | Won't be counted — see Dialer Discipline below |

### The Daily RPA Target

| Metric | Target | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Daily RPA total** | **≥ 300 minutes (5 hours)** | The activity floor for a full productive day |
| Available work time (9 AM – 5 PM minus lunch) | 7 hours | Your maximum |
| Required utilization | ~71% of work time | Leaves ~2 hours for breaks, notes, admin, transitions |

### How a Normal Day Reaches 300+

| Activity | Source | Typical Daily Time |
|---|---|---|
| Queue-ready time (logged in waiting for inbound) | ICD report | 60–90 min |
| Inbound talk time (4–5 conversations × ~15 min) | ICD report | 60–75 min |
| Outbound talk time (cadence + pool + follow-ups, combined) | CRM Calls Report | 100–140 min |
| Outbound dial overhead (total dials × ~30 sec ring/connect) | CRM Calls Report | 30–50 min |
| **Total RPAs** | | **~250–355 min** |

Hit the queues right and 300 takes care of itself. You don't have to count minutes — you just have to stay engaged on the company dialer.

### RPA Health Bands

| Daily RPAs | Status | What's Best |
|---|---|---|
| **≥ 300** | On the floor | You're putting in a full day |
| **240 – 299** | Light day | Not bad, but a couple hours got lost — review the day for gaps |
| **< 240** | Short shift | Activity is below what supports the lead allocation — let your manager know what got in the way |
| **≥ 360** | Strong | You're putting in extra effort — typically the agents at the top of the board |

### How RPAs Are Measured

Your daily RPA total is the sum of four numbers, drawn from two different scrapes that don't overlap. The CRM Calls Report captures everything you do *outbound*; the ICD report captures everything you do *inbound*. Adding them together gives the full picture.

```
Daily RPAs =
    queue_minutes           (ICD — Time Spent In Queue, you're logged in and available)
  + inbound_talk_minutes    (ICD — Total Talk Time, conversations from inbound calls)
  + outbound_talk_minutes   (CRM Calls Report — talk time on cadence, pool, follow-ups)
  + (outbound_dials × 0.5)  (CRM Calls Report — ring/connect overhead per outbound dial)
```

Two important notes:

- **No double-counting.** CRM Calls Report = pure outbound (all dials and talk that originated from your seat). ICD report = pure inbound (talk and queue time tied to leads coming to you). They're additive, not overlapping.
- **Logged-in time is the sum, not a separate field.** When you're in the queue, you're logged in. When you're talking, inbound or outbound, you're logged in. When you're dialing, you're logged in. The four numbers above *are* your logged-in time.

These roll up into a single RPA number on your daily dashboard — you don't have to track it manually.

### Quality Layer (Bonus Coaching Signal)

RPAs measure *time spent*. Two extra numbers on your dashboard measure *what came out of that time* — they don't change the 300 floor, but they tell you and your coach where your time is going:

| Marker | Source | What It Shows |
|---|---|---|
| **Conversations** (2–15 min calls) | CRM Daily Agent Performance | How many real talks you had — beyond just dial volume |
| **Presentations** (15+ min calls) | CRM Daily Agent Performance | How many full pitches you ran — the highest-value RPA minutes |
| **Pool long calls** (15+ min in pool) | CRM Pool Report | Pool-side presentation count |
| **Contact %** (`contacts_made / leads_worked`) | CRM Daily Agent Performance | Whether your dials are reaching people |

A day with 300 RPAs and 0 presentations is a coaching signal — you put in the time but didn't run real conversations. A day with 280 RPAs and 4 presentations is often a stronger sales day than 320 RPAs and 0.

## Queue Etiquette

There are four different "places" leads come from during your day, and each one has its own rhythm. Knowing which queue you're in — and treating it correctly — is the difference between a productive shift and a chaotic one. Most of this is just respect for the system and the other agents working alongside you.

### 1. Inbound Calls

These are leads the company routed live to your line.

- **Pick up.** Don't let it ring out. The lead is hot the moment they dial.
- Be ready. Headset on, CRM open, notes up before 9:00 AM.
- If you're on another call when an inbound rings, finish strong — don't rush the live conversation to grab the new one. The dialer will route it.
- After the call, **disposition immediately.** Don't stack 3–4 unworked leads on your screen.

### 2. Follow-Ups (Your Scheduled Callbacks)

These are appointments you set with prospects who asked you to call back.

- Treat them like a doctor's appointment — show up on time, every time.
- Make the call **at the scheduled time**, not 20 minutes later. The prospect remembers what time you said.
- One attempt is enough at the appointment. If they don't answer, leave a voicemail, drop a note, and move them back into your normal cadence.
- Don't double-book. If you set a 2:00 PM, don't also set a 2:05 PM with someone else.

### 3. Lead Queue (6-Touch Dialer Cadence)

This is the active dialer queue — leads the system is automatically cycling through 6 touches over ~8–9 days.

**Healthy queue size: around 60 leads.** Here's the math: 7 new leads/day × ~9 days in cadence ≈ 60 active leads at steady state. This is normal and the system handles it.

| Queue Size | Status | What's Best |
|---|---|---|
| **40 – 70** | Healthy / steady-state | The cadence is working. Stay the course. |
| **71 – 90** | Heavy | Touch attempts are spacing out — work through the queue actively, disposition stale leads |
| **> 90** | Backed up | Pause pool sessions, work cadence-eligible leads down to 70 |
| **< 30** | Light | You may be exiting leads too early, or new lead flow is low |

**Etiquette:**
- Let the cadence work. Don't manually re-dial leads the queue is already handling — it doubles up the prospect's experience and burns the lead.
- When a touch comes up, take it. Skipping touches makes the queue stale.
- If a lead has cycled through all 6 touches with no contact, **disposition it out** (or move to long-term follow-up). Don't park it.
- Keep dispositions current. Every lead you finish opens a slot for the next one.

### 4. Leads Pool (Shared)

This is the open pool — unassigned leads any agent can dial. Pool is **shared real estate**, so etiquette matters most here.

- **Only enter the pool when your own house is in order.** Past dues at zero, untouched at zero, follow-ups handled. Pool is supplemental.
- **Self-assign every answered contact** — interested, not interested, DNC, all of it. If you talked to them, take ownership so no one else dials them again. This is the single biggest courtesy you can extend to the rest of the team.
- Don't "cherry-pick." Skipping through pool leads at lightning speed to find an easy one wastes the lead and shows up in pool efficiency metrics. Dial what comes up.
- When you find 5 follow-up-worthy people, that's a great pool session. You can come back later if there's time.
- Be respectful of timing — pool is for the gaps between inbound, not a way to avoid taking inbound calls.

## Dialer Discipline

**All outbound calls go through the company dialer. No exceptions.**

The company dialer is what tracks your dials, talk time, cadence touches, and RPAs. Every call you make through it counts toward your activity, your numbers, and the data we use to coach you. Calls placed through any other channel (Dialpad, personal cell, hotspot phone, etc.) are invisible to the system — they don't count toward RPAs, they don't advance the cadence, and they break the lead's history.

### The Rules

- **All outbound dials → company dialer.** Cadence touches, follow-up callbacks, pool dials, manual dials, every one of them.
- **No outbound dialing through Dialpad.** It bypasses the cadence, breaks the lead history, and your dials don't count toward RPAs.
- **One acceptable exception:** A client occasionally calls into Dialpad directly (because they have your number from a prior conversation). When that happens, take the call — but flag it for your coach so the activity can be credited manually. Coaches verify these case-by-case.

### Why This Matters

| If you dial from... | What gets tracked |
|---|---|
| **Company dialer** | Dial counted, talk time counted, RPA credit, cadence advances, lead history complete |
| **Dialpad (outbound)** | Nothing — dial invisible, talk time invisible, no RPA credit, cadence stalls, lead history breaks |

If your numbers look low and you've actually been working hard, the first place to look is whether dials are leaking through Dialpad. The fix is simple: route everything through the company dialer.

## Inbound Calls — A Little More Detail

You get **7 inbound leads per day.** Each one is a real prospect who raised their hand — the single best thing you can do for your income is work each lead fully.

For every inbound lead:

1. Pick up.
2. Pitch.
3. Either close it, set a follow-up, or disposition it. Always one of those three by end of day.
4. If you set a follow-up, the appointment time is your commitment.

A 15% close rate (about 1 deal/day) is the target most agents reach when they work all 7 thoroughly through the full 6-touch cadence.

---

## Pool Sessions — A Little More Detail

Pool is where extra commission lives. It costs you nothing to dial it, and every pool sale is on top of your inbound earnings.

**The pool workflow — every session:**

1. Confirm your own house is in order first (untouched = 0, past due = 0).
2. Open a pool session in the dialer.
3. Dial.
4. **Self-assign every answered call** — even DNC and not-interested. Ownership is the cleanup.
5. For interested contacts, set a follow-up.
6. Close the session when you've found 5 follow-up-worthy people.

Tomorrow's warm callbacks come from today's pool work. The agents who consistently close pool sales are the ones who treated the pool seriously the day before.

## Pipeline Health (What You Personally Manage)

The lead queue (cadence) is automated — the system manages it. What you personally manage is a much smaller bucket: the leads that need a human touch *today*. Keep that bucket near zero and the rest of the system runs clean.

### Your Manual Workload = Untouched + Past Due

| Manual Workload | Status | What's Best |
|---|---|---|
| **0 – 3** | Clean | You're current — focus on inbound, then pool |
| **4 – 7** | Catch up | Knock these out before pool sessions today |
| **8+** | Needs focus | Pause pool, work this down before adding new activity |

### Untouched Leads (Brand-new, never dialed)

| Count | Best Move |
|---|---|
| **0** | Perfect — every new lead got a same-day touch |
| **1 – 3** | Touch them today, even if it's just a first attempt |
| **4+** | These are the freshest leads you have — make them the next thing you do |

### Past Due Follow-Ups (Missed appointments)

| Count | Best Move |
|---|---|
| **0** | You're current |
| **1 – 3** | Knock these out today before pool |
| **4+** | Make these the priority — they're warm leads going cold |

Past due follow-ups close at **2–3x the rate of fresh leads** because the prospect already raised their hand. Working them quickly is the highest-paying activity in your day.

## End-of-Day Checklist (5 Minutes)

Before you log out, take a quick pass through:

- [ ] RPA total ≥ 300 minutes (5 hours of queue-ready + dial + talk through the company dialer)
- [ ] Zero outbound dials placed through Dialpad
- [ ] All 7 inbound leads dispositioned
- [ ] Untouched leads at 0 (every new lead got a first touch)
- [ ] Past due follow-ups at 0 (or a plan to clear by tomorrow)
- [ ] Lead queue around 60 (or trending toward it)
- [ ] Pool session(s) wrapped, self-assigns logged
- [ ] Tomorrow's first callback on the calendar

A clean handoff at 5:00 PM means a calm start at 9:00 AM tomorrow.
