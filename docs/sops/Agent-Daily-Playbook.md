# Agent Daily Playbook

**Effective:** April 2026
**Read time:** 2 minutes
**Audience:** Every agent, every day

> Do these four things. The numbers follow.

---

## The Day in One Sentence

Work your follow-ups first, take your 7 inbound leads, then pull from the pool to build tomorrow's pipeline.

---

## Your 4 Daily Inputs

These are the only things you control. Hit them and the rest takes care of itself.

| # | Input | Daily Target | Where It's Tracked |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **RPAs** — minutes on the company dialer (queue-ready + dial + talk) | **≥ 300 / day (5 hrs)** | RPA Pacer |
| 2 | **Inbound leads worked** — full pitch, dispositioned by EOD | **7** | Lead Pacer |
| 3 | **Pool follow-ups found** — people worth a callback tomorrow | **5** | Pool tile |
| 4 | **Pool self-assign rate** — every answered pool call self-assigned | **≥ 30%** | Pool tile |

If those four are green at 5:00 PM, your day is a win.

---

## Outcomes These Inputs Produce

You don't manage these directly — they're what happens when the four inputs above are green. If an outcome drifts, look at the input that drives it.

| Outcome | Healthy | Driven By |
|---|---|---|
| Untouched leads (EOD) | **0** | Input #1 (RPAs — you had time to touch them) |
| Past due follow-ups (EOD) | **0** | Inputs #1 + #2 (you showed up and worked the day) |
| Active lead queue | **~60 steady** | Inputs #1 + #2 working over 8–9 days |
| Sales / day | **1** (≈15% close on inbound) | Inputs #1 + #2 |
| Pool sales / week | **1** | Inputs #3 + #4 |

---

## End-of-Day Checklist (5 min, before logout)

- [ ] **300+ RPAs** on the company dialer
- [ ] **0** outbound dials placed through Dialpad
- [ ] **All 7 inbound leads** dispositioned (close, follow-up, or out)
- [ ] **0 untouched** leads remaining
- [ ] **0 past due** follow-ups remaining
- [ ] **5 pool follow-ups** set for tomorrow
- [ ] **Tomorrow's first callback** on the calendar

A clean handoff at 5:00 PM means a calm 9:00 AM tomorrow.

---

## Three Rules That Never Change

1. **All outbound dials go through the company dialer.** Dialpad outbounds don't count toward RPAs, don't advance the cadence, and break the lead history.
2. **Honor every appointment at the scheduled time.** The prospect remembers what time you said. One attempt, voicemail if no answer, then back into cadence.
3. **Self-assign every pool answered call** — including DNC and not-interested. Ownership is how the pool stays clean for the team.

---

## When Things Look Off

| Symptom | First Place to Look |
|---|---|
| Sales low, RPAs high | Input #2 — are you actually working all 7 leads, or stacking them? Check Conversations + Presentations on your dashboard |
| RPAs low, you feel busy | Dialpad leakage. Every outbound goes through the company dialer or it's invisible |
| Past due piling up | Input #1 — you're not on the dialer long enough to honor your appointments |
| Pool sales dry | Input #4 — if self-assign is below 30%, you're not actually owning pool contacts |
| Queue > 75 | Work cadence-eligible leads down before opening pool |

---

## The Mindset

The 6-touch cadence does the heavy lifting in the background.
Your job is three things:

1. Live inbound conversations
2. Honoring the follow-ups you've set
3. Keeping the small manual backlog (untouched + past due) at zero

Stay engaged on the company dialer for 5 hours of real activity (RPAs) and the production follows.
