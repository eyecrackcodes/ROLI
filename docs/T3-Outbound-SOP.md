# T3 Outbound — Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026

Your daily playbook — what to do, when to do it, and what your numbers should look like.

---

## The Three Activity Channels

### Channel 1: Follow-Up Appointments

Scheduled callbacks you set from prior conversations. You call manually at the scheduled time — these are not in the dialer.

**The 1-5 Close rule:** Set every appointment within 24-48 hours. Interest fades fast. Close within 1-5 total contacts.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Daily follow-ups | 2-8 |
| Dials per follow-up | 1 attempt at scheduled time |
| Expected dials | 2-8/day |
| Expected talk time | 15-45 min |
| Past due tolerance | **0** |

**If they answer:** Qualify, present, close, or reschedule within 24-48 hours.

**If they don't answer:** Sticky note in CRM with context, then move the lead into a contact attempt so the dialer picks it up. Don't manually redial — let the dialer handle it.

**Past due must be zero.** If it's rising, stop pool activity and clear follow-ups first.

### Channel 2: Queue Dialer (6-Attempt Cadence)

Your personal assigned leads. The dialer cycles through them automatically.

Each lead gets **6 contact attempts max**, then withdraw. If a lead is DNC, wrong number, or no longer reachable — withdraw immediately.

Your queue is a mix of pool self-assigns, IB/OB assignments, and missed appointments you moved to the dialer. Check the sticky note before each connected call.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Queue dials | 90-130/day |
| Expected talk time | 60-90 min |
| Healthy queue size | 50-120 leads |
| Max attempts per lead | 6, then withdraw |
| Queue delta d/d | Stable or declining |

If queue exceeds 150, you're not removing leads that should be gone.

### Channel 3: Leads Pool (Self-Assign + Clean)

Shared leads — nobody owns them. When someone answers, you self-assign them to pull them out of the pool. Self-assigning is the only way a lead leaves the pool.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Pool dials | 70-100/day |
| Expected talk time | 60-90 min |
| Long calls (15+ min) | ≥ 4/day |
| Self-assign rate | ≥ 30% of pool answered |
| Pool % of total dials | 25-40% |

**Contact rate** = % of pool dials where someone answers. At 50%, half your dials connect to a live person.

**Voicemail rule:** Hang up and move on. You don't own pool leads — no reason to leave a message. If you must, use the **automatic VM drop**. Manual voicemails inflate your talk time and contact rate.

### Block 1 — Follow-Up Appointments (9:00 - 9:30 AM)

- Check task list, call each appointment — one attempt
- If they answer: work the call
- If no answer: sticky note + move to queue
- **Expected:** 2-8 dials, 15-45 min talk time

### Block 2 — Queue Dialer (9:30 AM - 2:30 PM)

- Run dialer through your assigned queue
- Check notes before each connected call
- Withdraw leads at 6th failed attempt
- **Expected:** 90-130 dials, 60-90 min talk time

### Block 3 — Pool Session (2:45 - 5:00 PM)

- Dial pool leads
- When someone answers: self-assign and disposition immediately
- When you hit voicemail: hang up, next dial
- Target 4+ long calls (15+ min)
- **Expected:** 70-100 dials, 60-90 min talk time

## Self-Assignment Rules

Self-assign **every** answered pool contact.

| Outcome | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| DNC | Self-assign → mark DNC | Removes lead from rotation |
| Wrong Number | Self-assign → note it | Removes bad data from pool |
| Not Interested | Self-assign → note reason | Stops the lead from being re-dialed |
| Voicemail / No Answer | Do NOT assign | Lead stays in pool for future attempts |
| Callback Requested | Self-assign → set follow-up date | Lead moves to YOUR appointments |
| Interested | Self-assign → schedule follow-up | Lead becomes your prospect |
| Sale | Self-assign → submit application | Close it |

## Queue Discipline: The 6-Attempt Rule

This applies to **your lead queue only** — not the leads pool.

6 contact attempts max per lead. After the 6th, withdraw. DNC or no longer reachable on any attempt — withdraw right away.

### Queue Throughput

| Queue Size | Daily Dials | Days to Exhaust | Withdrawal Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 leads | ~50/day | 6 days | ~8 leads/day |
| 80 leads | ~80/day | 6 days | ~13 leads/day |
| 120 leads | ~120/day | 6 days | ~20 leads/day |

### Queue Health

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Queue < 50 | Light | Increase pool time for more self-assigns |
| Queue 50-120 | Healthy | Maintain current balance |
| Queue 120-200 | Accumulating | Audit queue, enforce withdrawals |
| Queue > 200 | Overloaded | Stop pool, clear and withdraw old leads |

## Pipeline Flow

You arrive at T3 with a full pipeline from prior tiers. Pool is layered on top.

```
Your existing pipeline:
  ├── 5-10 daily follow-up appointments
  ├── 50-120 leads in your call queue
  └── Active prospects in various stages

What pool adds each day:
  80 pool dials → 40 answered → 12+ self-assigned
    ├── 6-8 dispositioned (DNC/NI/wrong#) → removed from pool
    ├── 4-5 callbacks → added to YOUR follow-up appointments
    ├── 1-2 interested → added to YOUR call queue
    └── 0-1 sale
```

### Weekly Targets

Full daily targets from Week 1. Pool is additive to your existing pipeline.

| Week | Pool Self-Assigns/Day | Queue Leads | Daily Follow-Ups | Queue Dials | Pool Dials | Total Dials |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 10-15 | 80-120 | 5-10 | 110-130 | 70-100 | 200+ |
| Week 2 | 10-15 | 80-130 | 6-12 | 110-130 | 70-90 | 200+ |
| Week 3+ | 10-15 | 80-120 steady | 6-12 | 110-130 | 70-90 | 200+ |

By Week 2, pool self-assigns start generating their own follow-up appointments. Missed appointments flow into the queue with sticky notes. Enforce the 6-attempt rule to keep queue at 120 or below.

## Red Flags

| Pattern | Signal | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| High dials, low talk time | 200+ dials, < 100 min talk | Speed-dialing without engaging |
| Past due rising | Past due > 0 and growing | Missing scheduled appointments |
| Queue growing | Queue > 150 and increasing | Not withdrawing after 6 attempts |
| Pool heavy + queue neglected | Pool > 40% + queue > 120 | Avoiding pipeline work |
| Assigns without engagement | High assign rate, 0 long calls | Checking the box without real conversations |
