# T3 Outbound — Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026

This is your daily playbook. It covers everything you're expected to do across your three work types — follow-up appointments, queue dialer, and leads pool — and what the numbers should look like at the end of the day.

---

## The Three Activity Channels

T3 outbound activity splits into three distinct channels, each with different mechanics and expectations.

### Channel 1: Follow-Up Appointments (Task-Date Driven)

Scheduled callbacks the agent created from prior contacts. These are NOT in the dialer — the agent manually calls at the task-date time. Highest-value activity because the lead was already contacted and an appointment was set.

**The 1-5 Close rule:** Always set appointments within 24-48 hours of a conversation. Interest fades fast — every day you wait, the lead gets colder. The goal is to close within 1-5 total contacts.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Daily follow-ups | 2-8 (varies by pipeline maturity) |
| Dials per follow-up | 1 attempt at the scheduled time |
| Expected dials | 2-8/day |
| Expected talk time | 15-45 min |
| Past due tolerance | **0** |

**How to handle each appointment:**

1. Call the lead at the scheduled time.
2. **If they answer:** Have the conversation — qualify, present, close, or reschedule within 24-48 hours.
3. **If they don't answer:** Add a sticky note in the CRM with your context (what you discussed, what they were interested in, where you left off). Then move the lead into a contact attempt so the dialer picks it up in its natural cadence. Do NOT manually redial 2-3 times — that's what the dialer is built for.

This keeps past due at zero. You worked the appointment — the lead just moved from a manual channel to the dialer where it gets systematic follow-up instead of ad hoc redialing. The sticky note means your context is preserved for when they do connect through the queue.

**Rule:** Past due follow-ups must be zero. If past due is rising, the agent is either skipping appointments or not moving no-answers into the queue. Stop pool activity and clear follow-ups first.

### Channel 2: Queue Dialer (6-Attempt Cadence)

Your lead queue is **your personal list** — every lead in it is assigned to you. The dialer automatically cycles through it. You control session start/stop and call quality.

Each lead in your queue gets a maximum of **6 contact attempts**. After the 6th attempt without meaningful contact, **withdraw the lead** from your queue. If a lead is DNC, wrong number, or otherwise dead, withdraw it immediately — don't wait for 6 attempts.

Your queue contains a mix of leads: pool self-assigns you pulled, inbound/outbound assignments, and missed follow-up appointments you moved to the dialer. Some of these are warm — check the sticky note before each connected call.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Queue dials | 90-130/day |
| Expected talk time | 60-90 min |
| Healthy queue size | 50-120 leads |
| Max attempts per lead | 6, then withdraw |
| Queue delta d/d | Stable or declining |

**Cadence math:** At 1 attempt per lead per day, a queue of 100 leads produces ~100 dials/day. Each lead completes 6 attempts in 6 business days, then exits. New leads from pool self-assigns (4-8/day), missed appointment conversions (1-3/day), and IB/OB assignment (5-10/day) keep the queue flowing.

**Queue health:** If queue exceeds 150, leads are accumulating without withdrawal. If queue delta is positive for 3+ consecutive days, you're not removing leads that should be gone.

### Channel 3: Leads Pool (Self-Assign + Clean)

The leads pool is a **shared bucket** — nobody owns these leads. When you dial into the pool and someone answers, you self-assign them to pull them out of the pool. That's the only way a lead leaves the pool. There are no contact attempts or withdrawals here like in your queue — self-assigning IS the removal.

| Metric | Expectation |
|---|---|
| Pool dials | 70-100/day |
| Expected talk time | 60-90 min |
| Long calls (15+ min) | ≥ 4/day |
| Self-assign rate | ≥ 30% of pool answered |
| Pool % of total dials | 25-40% |

**Contact rate in the pool** = the percentage of your pool dials where a person actually answers. A 50% contact rate means half your dials connect to a live person. This is the number your self-assigns, long calls, and sales all flow from — more contacts means more opportunities.

**Voicemail rule:** Do not leave voicemails in the pool. These are unassigned leads — there is no reason to leave a message for someone you don't own yet. When you hit voicemail, hang up and move to the next dial. If you do choose to leave a voicemail, use the **automatic VM drop** so the system ends the call immediately. A manual voicemail counts as talk time and inflates your contact rate, which makes your numbers look different from your actual results.

## Daily Workflow

### Block 1 — Follow-Up Appointments (AM / Scheduled Times)

Before anything else, work all scheduled follow-ups. These are people who already talked to you and are expecting your call — highest close probability.

- Check your task list for today's appointments
- Call each lead at the scheduled time — one attempt
- If they answer: qualify, present, close, or reschedule within 24-48 hours
- If they don't answer: sticky note in CRM with context, move to queue for dialer follow-up
- **Expected:** 2-8 dials, 15-45 min talk time

### Block 2 — Queue Dialer Session (AM–Early PM)

Start the regular dialer session. The system cycles through your assigned queue automatically. Some of these leads are warm — check the sticky note before each connected call so you have context.

- Run dialer through assigned pipeline
- Each connected call: check notes, qualify, and disposition
- Withdraw leads at 6th failed attempt
- **Expected:** 90-130 dials, 60-90 min talk time

### Block 3 — Pool Session (PM)

Switch to the leads pool dialer. New lead acquisition.

- Dial through pool leads
- When someone answers: talk to them, self-assign, and disposition immediately
- When you hit voicemail: hang up and move on — do not leave a message
- Set follow-ups in real time — don't batch for later
- Target 4+ long calls (15+ min presentations)
- **Expected:** 70-100 dials, 60-90 min talk time

**Note:** Self-assigns are dispositioned immediately after each call, not saved for end of day. If someone wants a callback, set the follow-up date right then. If you made a sale, submit the app right then.

## Self-Assignment Rules

Self-assign **every** answered pool contact. This is non-negotiable.

| Outcome | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| DNC | Self-assign → mark DNC | Removes lead from rotation |
| Wrong Number | Self-assign → note it | Removes bad data from pool |
| Not Interested | Self-assign → note reason | Stops the lead from being re-dialed |
| Voicemail / No Answer | Do NOT assign | Lead stays in pool for future attempts |
| Callback Requested | Self-assign → set follow-up date | Lead moves to YOUR appointments |
| Interested | Self-assign → schedule follow-up | Lead becomes your active prospect |
| Sale | Self-assign → submit application | Close it |

## Queue Discipline: The 6-Attempt Rule

This applies to **your lead queue only** — not the leads pool. Every lead in your queue is assigned to you and gets a maximum of 6 contact attempts through the dialer. After the 6th attempt without meaningful contact, **withdraw the lead**. If a lead turns out to be DNC, wrong number, or dead on any attempt, withdraw it right away.

### Queue Throughput Model

| Queue Size | Daily Dials | Days to Exhaust | Withdrawal Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 leads | ~50/day | 6 days | ~8 leads/day |
| 80 leads | ~80/day | 6 days | ~13 leads/day |
| 120 leads | ~120/day | 6 days | ~20 leads/day |

### Queue Health Signals

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Queue < 50 | Light load | Increase pool time for more self-assigns |
| Queue 50-120 | Healthy | Maintain current balance |
| Queue 120-200 | Accumulating | Audit queue, enforce withdrawals |
| Queue > 200 | Bloated — leads rotting | Stop pool, clear and withdraw stale leads |

## Pipeline Flow: How Pool Feeds Pipeline

T3 agents arrive with an existing pipeline from their time in prior tiers — follow-up appointments, queue leads, and active prospects are already in place. Pool is the new channel layered on top. From day one, pool self-assigns add to an already-full workload.

```
Your existing pipeline (from T1/T2):
  ├── 5-10 daily follow-up appointments (warm callbacks)
  ├── 50-120 leads in your call queue (assigned pipeline)
  └── Active prospects in various stages

What pool adds each day:
  80 pool dials → 40 answered → 12+ self-assigned
    ├── 6-8 dispositioned (DNC/NI/wrong#) → removed from pool
    ├── 4-5 set callback → added to YOUR follow-up appointments
    ├── 1-2 interested → added to YOUR call queue
    └── 0-1 sale → submit app
```

Pool contacts flow into your existing pipeline. Tomorrow you have a few more follow-ups and a few more queue leads than today. The pool keeps your pipeline full as older leads close out or get withdrawn.

### Expected Weekly Targets

You should be hitting full daily targets from Week 1. Your pipeline is already built — pool is additive.

| Week | Pool Self-Assigns/Day | Queue Leads | Daily Follow-Ups | Queue Dials | Pool Dials | Total Dials |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | 10-15 | 80-120 | 5-10 | 110-130 | 70-100 | 200+ |
| Week 2 | 10-15 | 80-130 | 6-12 | 110-130 | 70-90 | 200+ |
| Week 3+ | 10-15 | 80-120 steady | 6-12 | 110-130 | 70-90 | 200+ |

By Week 2, pool self-assigns from the first week start generating their own follow-up appointments, adding to the ones you brought from prior tiers. Missed appointments flow into the queue with sticky notes, so queue quality improves over time as it includes warm leads alongside cold pipeline. Enforce the 6-attempt withdrawal rule to keep queue size at 120 or below.
