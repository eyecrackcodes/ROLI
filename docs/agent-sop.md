# DSB Remote Team — Agent Standard Operating Procedure

**Effective:** April 2026
**Model:** Unified Remote (all agents RMT)
**Lead allocation:** 7 inbound leads/day at ~$55-60/lead

---

## Daily Schedule (Central Time)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 9:00 AM | Log in. Review pipeline: work any past due follow-ups first. |
| 9:00–12:00 | Inbound lead block. Take calls, work leads as they come in. |
| 12:00–1:00 | Follow-up appointments from yesterday's pool contacts. |
| 1:00–3:00 | Inbound + pool sessions. If inbound is slow, get in pool. |
| 3:00–4:00 | Pool block. Find 5 people to follow up with tomorrow. |
| 4:00–5:00 | Final inbound block. Close out pipeline notes. Clean CRM. |

The schedule is flexible based on inbound volume. The non-negotiable is: **past due follow-ups get worked before anything else, every day.**

---

## Performance Floor (Minimum Expectations)

These are not goals. These are the floor. Falling below any of these triggers coaching intervention.

| Metric | Floor | How it's measured |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| **Close Rate** | **20%** weekly | Sales / leads delivered (Mon–today) |
| **Pipeline Size** | **≤ 30 leads** | Past due + new leads + today's follow-ups |
| **Past Due Follow-ups** | **0** | Must be worked daily. No exceptions. |
| **Pool Self-assigns** | **5 per day** | Self-assign every answered contact in pool |
| **Pool Sales** | **1 per week** | At least one close from pool contacts weekly |
| **Pool Assign Rate** | **≥ 30%** | Self-assign / answered calls in pool |

### Bonus Lead Eligibility

Agents who maintain **22%+ CR** with a clean pipeline (≤20 leads, 0 past due) are eligible for additional inbound leads beyond the standard 7/day.

---

## Close Rate: Why 20% Is the Floor

Every lead costs the company ~$55-60. At 7 leads/day, that is **$385-420/day** invested in each agent.

| Weekly CR | Leads (35/wk) | Sales | Cost per sale | Verdict |
|-----------|---------------|-------|---------------|---------|
| 10% | 35 | 3.5 | $600 | Losing money |
| 15% | 35 | 5.25 | $400 | Break-even at best |
| 20% | 35 | 7 | $300 | Sustainable |
| 25% | 35 | 8.75 | $240 | Profitable |

At 20%, the business can sustain your leads. Below 20%, the company is spending more to acquire your leads than the revenue justifies. This is not about effort — it is about whether your conversion covers the cost of the leads you receive.

## Pipeline Discipline

Your pipeline is **paid inventory**. Every lead in it cost money to acquire.

### What each status means

| Status | What to do |
|--------|-----------|
| **Past due follow-ups** | These are callbacks you scheduled and missed. Work them NOW. Every past due lead is money aging out. |
| **Today's follow-ups** | Appointments scheduled for today. Call at the scheduled time. One attempt. If no answer, sticky note and move to queue. |
| **New leads** | Unworked leads assigned to you. Touch them within 24 hours or they go stale. |
| **Call queue** | Leads in dialer rotation. If queue exceeds 150, withdraw leads that have had 6+ attempts. |

### Pipeline limits

| Pipeline size | What happens |
|---------------|-------------|
| ≤ 20 | Healthy. Room for inbound + pool follow-ups. |
| 21–30 | At capacity. Work existing pipeline before adding more. |
| 31–40 | Over limit. Clear pipeline before pool or additional inbound. |
| > 40 | Critical. Stop all new activity. Work pipeline down immediately. |

## Leads Pool

The pool is not optional filler. It is where you **build tomorrow's follow-up pipeline**.

### What to do in pool

1. Enter pool during designated blocks (after follow-up appointments are complete)
2. Dial contacts
3. **Self-assign every answered contact** — including not interested and DNC — to remove them from rotation
4. Find **5 people** each day who are worth following up with tomorrow
5. Target **1 pool sale per week**

### Why self-assigning matters

Unassigned answered contacts cycle back into the pool and get called again by someone else. That is wasted effort for the team. Self-assigning cleans the pool and gives you ownership of the contact for follow-up.

| Assign Rate | Assessment |
|-------------|-----------|
| ≥ 50% | Excellent — fully cleaning pool contacts |
| 30–49% | Compliant — pool is moving |
| < 30% | Non-compliant — contacts recycling back into pool |

## What Gets Tracked (and How You're Coached)

ROLI monitors your performance hourly and generates a coaching recommendation. Here is what triggers each action:

| Recommendation | What triggers it | What to do |
|----------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Work follow-ups** | Past due > 0 | Clear your past due appointments before anything else |
| **Clear pipeline** | Pipeline > 30 | Close out, withdraw, or reschedule stale leads |
| **Review quality** | CR below 15% for 3+ straight days | Manager will audit your calls. Review your close technique and presentations. |
| **Focus pipeline** | Weekly CR below 20% | Stop taking new leads. Convert what you have. Review call quality. |
| **Pick up pace** | Dials well below team median | Maintain steady calling cadence. Pipeline is clean — keep dialing. |
| **Get in pool** | < 5 pool self-assigns by midday | Get in pool and find contacts to follow up with tomorrow |
| **Stay in queue** | CR 20-21%, pipeline healthy | Keep working inbound. Close more to hit bonus-lead eligibility. |
| **Take more leads** | CR ≥ 22%, pipeline ≤ 20, past due 0 | You're ready for additional volume |

## Cost Awareness

Every lead has a cost. Every missed follow-up is money wasted.

- **Lead cost:** ~$55-60 per lead (marketing CPC)
- **Daily investment per agent:** ~$385-420 (7 leads × ~$55-60)
- **Weekly investment per agent:** ~$1,925-2,100
- **Break-even close rate:** ~18-20% (depends on avg premium)

When ROLI shows your cost-per-sale, that number should ideally be **under $300**. If it is over $500, the company is losing money on your leads.

---

## What Success Looks Like

An agent performing well:
- Closes 20%+ of leads received (minimum 7 sales per 35 weekly leads)
- Has 0 past due follow-ups at the end of every day
- Pipeline stays under 30 leads
- Self-assigns 5+ pool contacts daily
- Closes 1+ pool sale per week
- Cost per sale under $300
- Responsive to coaching recommendations

An agent eligible for growth:
- All of the above, plus CR ≥ 22%
- Clean pipeline (≤ 20 leads)
- Consistent week over week (no 3-day CR dips below 15%)
- Ready for additional lead volume

## Feedback

If something is broken — a tool, a process, a script, a contracting issue — say something immediately. Do not wait for a meeting. The company was built on feedback and wants to hear what is not working every day.

Channels:
- Direct message your manager
- Post in the team Slack channel
- Raise it in the daily huddle

The faster friction is identified, the faster it gets fixed.
